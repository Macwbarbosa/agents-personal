## INTRODUCTION

FLAG stands for **Forest, Land and Agriculture**. The FLAG sector is the **third highest emitting sector** globally. Agriculture, Forestry and Other Land Use (AFOLU) accounts for approximately **22% of global GHG emissions**.

**FLAG science-based targets (SBTs) are ADDITIONAL to cross-sector SBTs.** Companies operating in the FLAG sector must set two types of targets:
1. A cross-sector science-based target (covering energy/industry emissions)
2. A FLAG science-based target (covering FLAG-related emissions)

FLAG targets cannot be combined into a single target with cross-sector targets; they must be separate.

### Why FLAG Targets?

The land sector has enormous mitigation potential — approximately **12 GtCO₂e/yr** by 2050. This mitigation potential must be captured to limit global warming to 1.5°C. Land sector emissions are distinct from fossil fuel emissions: they arise from land use change (LUC), agriculture, and forestry, and also include land-based carbon removals (sinks).

### Three FLAG Target Approaches

Companies may use one of three approaches:

1. **FLAG Sector Pathway**: An economy-wide, demand-side approach. Annual absolute reduction of **3.03% per year** from the base year. Covers all FLAG emissions.

2. **FLAG Commodity Pathways**: Production-intensity approach based on specific commodities. Uses commodity-specific annual reduction rates (see Table 8 in Section 4.1).

3. **Mixed approach**: A company may set targets using the FLAG Sector Pathway for some FLAG emission sources and commodity pathways for others, provided the full FLAG emission boundary is covered.

## SECTION 2: REQUIREMENTS FOR SETTING FLAG TARGETS

### FLAG-C1: Setting a FLAG SBT (Revised V1.2)

**Companies must set FLAG SBTs if:**
- FLAG emissions are **≥ 20%** of total scope 1, 2, and 3 GHG emissions; OR
- FLAG scope 1 + scope 2 emissions are **≥ 20%** of total scope 1 + scope 2 emissions.

**Exemptions:**
- Companies where FLAG emissions were previously material but are now **≤ 5%** of total emissions are exempt.
- Companies where FLAG emissions are **< 20%** of total emissions are **encouraged** (but not required) to set FLAG SBTs.
- Companies with **no FLAG emissions** are not required to set FLAG SBTs.

**Timing:**
- Companies must submit FLAG SBTs within the timeframe specified by SBTi criteria at the time of submission.
- Existing commitments made before the V1.2 revision remain subject to the criteria applicable at the time of commitment.

### FLAG-C2: Scope and Boundary of FLAG SBTs

**FLAG SBTs must include:**
- All material FLAG-related emissions sources within the company's organizational boundary.
- Scope 1 and scope 2 FLAG emissions (mandatory).
- Scope 3 FLAG emissions if they represent ≥ 40% of the company's total scope 1 + 2 + 3 FLAG emissions.
- Land Use Change (LUC) emissions (mandatory where material).
- Land management emissions (mandatory where material).

**Emission sources within FLAG scope include:**
- Enteric fermentation (livestock)
- Manure management
- Rice cultivation
- Agricultural soils (synthetic fertilizers, organic amendments)
- Prescribed burning of savannas
- Field burning of agricultural residues
- LUC (deforestation, peatland drainage, conversion)
- Forestry and other land use
- Bioenergy feedstock production

**FLAG emissions are distinct from:**
- Scope 1 fossil fuel combustion (energy)
- Scope 2 purchased energy
- Non-FLAG scope 3 categories (e.g., employee commuting, business travel)

### FLAG-C3: GHG Accounting Standards

**Before January 1, 2027:** Companies may use:
- GHG Protocol Agricultural Guidance (WRI/WBCSD)
- Equivalent GHG accounting standards approved by SBTi

**Effective January 1, 2027:** Companies **must use** the:
- **GHG Protocol Land Sector and Removals Standard** (Version 1.0, 2026)

This standard covers both emissions and removals from land, forests, soils, and agriculture. It is the comprehensive accounting guidance for FLAG targets going forward.

### FLAG-C4: No-Deforestation Commitment (Revised V1.2)

Companies subject to FLAG-C1 **must commit to eliminating deforestation** from their operations and supply chains.

**Target date requirements:**
- The no-deforestation target date must be **no later than 2 years** after submission of the FLAG SBT.
- **Absolute deadline: December 31, 2030** (no company may set a no-deforestation target date later than December 31, 2030).
- Companies with a prior commitment to achieve no-deforestation by 2025 **may not set a new commitment date later than December 31, 2028.**

**Cutoff date (deforestation baseline date):**
- Best practice: **≤ 2020** or **≤ 3 years before the first FLAG SBT submission** (whichever is earlier).
- Cutoff dates are fixed and **cannot be moved to a later date** once set.
- The cutoff date defines the baseline against which deforestation compliance is measured.

**Scope of no-deforestation commitment:**
- Must cover **primary deforestation-linked commodities** (see Appendix A / Table A.1).
- These include: cattle, cocoa, coffee, oil palm, rubber, soy, and timber.
- Includes volumes contained in transformed products (where the commodity makes up ≥ 1% of the finished sourced product).
- Includes feed embedded in animal products.

**Implementing the commitment:**
- Companies must demonstrate progress toward their no-deforestation commitment through monitoring and transparent reporting.
- **Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC)** is required for mitigation implementation activities affecting Indigenous Peoples and Local Communities.
- Jurisdictional approaches may complement supply chain commitments.

### FLAG-C5: Absolute FLAG Targets (FLAG Sector Pathway)

**FLAG Sector Pathway:**
- Annual absolute reduction rate: **3.03% per year** from the base year.
- This is a demand-side, economy-wide pathway based on Roe et al. (2019).
- Covers all FLAG-related emissions (scope 1+2 FLAG and, if applicable, scope 3 FLAG).
- Base year emissions × (1 - 0.0303)^(target year - base year) = target year emissions.

**Example calculation:**
If base year is 2020 and target year is 2030:
Reduction = 3.03% × 10 years = 30.3% total reduction (compound).

### FLAG-C6: Intensity FLAG Targets (Commodity Pathways)

**FLAG Commodity Pathways** are production-intensity pathways based on specific commodities. Companies choosing this approach must set targets for each commodity they produce or source that falls within their FLAG boundary.

**Required reduction rates per commodity per year (compound annual rate):**

| Commodity | Annual Reduction Rate (Physical Intensity) |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------|
| Beef | 2.40% per year |
| Chicken | 3.90% per year |
| Dairy | 3.10% per year |
| Leather | 2.50% per year |
| Maize | 3.50% per year |
| Palm Oil | 3.10% per year |
| Pork | 3.30% per year |
| Rice | 2.90% per year |
| Soy | 3.80% per year |
| Timber & Wood Fiber | 2.80% per year |
| Wheat | 3.60% per year |

**Intensity metric:** Emissions per tonne of commodity produced or sourced (physical intensity approach). The metrics are defined in the FLAG Tool and methods addendum.

**Source:** FLAG commodity pathways developed from Smith et al. (2016) using the IMAGE 3.0 Integrated Assessment Model. Timber & wood fiber pathway developed by Quantis using FAO Forest Resources Assessment (FRA) and scientific literature.

### FLAG-C7: Mixed FLAG Target Approach

A company may set targets using:
- The FLAG Sector Pathway for **some** FLAG emission sources, AND
- Commodity pathways for **other** FLAG emission sources.

Provided:
- The full FLAG emission boundary is covered (no gaps).
- The mixed approach is transparently documented.
- Each part of the mixed approach meets the relevant pathway requirements (C5 or C6).

Companies must use the **SBTi FLAG Tool** (provided free by SBTi) to calculate FLAG SBTs.

- Companies should use the highest quality data available.
- Primary data (directly measured) is preferred over secondary data (emission factors, databases).

- Data sources must be documented and disclosed.

### FLAG-C9: Target Language

**Box 2: Required FLAG SBT Language Templates**

**For absolute FLAG targets (FLAG Sector Pathway):**
> "[Company name] commits to reduce absolute [scopes] FLAG GHG emissions [X]% by [target year] from a [base year] base year. Target includes FLAG emissions and removals."

**For intensity FLAG targets (commodity pathways):**
> "[Company name] commits to reduce [scopes] FLAG GHG emissions [X]% per [unit of production] by [target year] from a [base year] base year. Target boundary includes FLAG emissions and removals associated with [commodity/commodities]."

**Notes on target language:**
- "Scopes" must specify which scopes are covered (e.g., "scope 1, 2, and 3 FLAG").
- The phrase "Target includes FLAG emissions and removals" is mandatory where land-based removals are included in the target boundary.
- Where a company uses the biogenic accounting footnote (per NT C10), the following must be added: *"The target boundary includes land-related emissions and removals from biogenic feedstocks."*

### FLAG-C10: Bioenergy

Companies that use bioenergy feedstocks must:
- Report CO₂ emissions AND removals from biogenic feedstocks separately from fossil GHG.
- Include **net biogenic CO₂** in the FLAG target boundary.
- Add the footnote: *"The target boundary includes land-related emissions and removals from biogenic feedstocks."*
- Account for both the biogenic CO₂ released in combustion and the carbon sequestered during feedstock growth.

### FLAG-C11: Land Management and Carbon Removals

**Land management changes that reduce FLAG emissions** (e.g., improved agricultural practices, reduced tillage, better manure management) **count toward FLAG SBTs**.

**Carbon removals from land** (e.g., afforestation, reforestation, soil carbon enhancement):
- **Do NOT count toward FLAG near-term SBTs.**
- May count toward **net-zero targets** and **BVCM (Beyond Value Chain Mitigation)**.
- May be used for neutralization of residual emissions under the Corporate Net-Zero Standard.

**FPIC requirement:**
- Companies must obtain **Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC)** from affected Indigenous Peoples and Local Communities before implementing any FLAG mitigation measures on or affecting their lands.

### FLAG-C12: FLAG Criteria Review Cycle

- FLAG criteria are reviewed **every 2 years**.
- Companies must follow the most recent version of FLAG criteria.
- A **6-month grace period** applies after a new version is published before mandatory compliance.
- Companies with active targets validated under previous versions must update to new criteria during the next mandatory 5-year review or upon resubmission.

## SECTION 3: GUIDANCE

### 3.1 LUC Emissions (Land Use Change)

**Types of LUC:**
- **dLUC (direct LUC):** Emissions from land-use change directly caused by a specific company's operations or supply chain (e.g., a company clearing forest to grow soy).
- **sLUC (statistical LUC):** LUC emissions allocated statistically based on commodity production volumes and regional LUC data. Used as a proxy for dLUC where direct measurement is unavailable.
- **iLUC (indirect LUC):** Emissions displaced to other locations due to changes in land use elsewhere (e.g., production shifting to previously forested land). These are not currently required in FLAG accounting.

**sLUC as proxy for dLUC:** The SBTi FLAG methodology uses sLUC as a proxy for dLUC. This approach allocates historical LUC emissions to commodities produced in deforestation-linked regions, proportional to production volumes.

**20-year LUC assessment period:**
Deforestation causes GHG emissions (e.g., from biomass and soils) that extend beyond the year trees are cut. The SBTi FLAG methodology allocates deforestation emissions **linearly over 20 years** following a deforestation event. This is based on:
- IPCC Good Practice Guidance for LULUCF (Penman et al., 2003)
- GHG Protocol Land Sector and Removals Standard (GHG Protocol, 2026)

**Key fact:** Deforestation represents **12% of global emissions** and **50% of AFOLU emissions** (Roe et al., 2019). The FLAG sector pathway includes LUC associated with conversion of natural ecosystems (contributing ~1 GtCO₂e/yr additional mitigation beyond the 3.6 GtCO₂e/yr from agricultural LUC).

### 3.2 No-Deforestation Commitments

*(See FLAG-C4 above for the full requirements.)*

**Additional guidance:**
- The Accountability Framework initiative (AFi) provides guidance on deforestation-free commitments and supply chain due diligence.
- Companies should work with suppliers on monitoring, verification, and transparency.
- Satellite monitoring tools (e.g., Global Forest Watch, Trase) can support commitment implementation.
- Jurisdictional approaches (REDD+, jurisdictional deforestation-free certification) may complement but not replace supply chain commitments.

**Why deforestation must stop as soon as possible:**
The FLAG guidance introduces criteria for no-deforestation goals because the FLAG sector and FLAG commodity pathway models achieve zero deforestation by 2030 (as per Figure 13). No-deforestation commitments are necessary to align corporate action with the trajectory embedded in the FLAG science.

### 3.3 Land Management Emissions

**What counts as land management in FLAG:**
Changes to agricultural and forest management practices that reduce GHG emissions, including:
- Improved livestock management (reducing enteric methane)
- Manure management improvements
- Fertilizer use efficiency improvements
- Rice management changes (intermittent flooding vs. continuous flooding)
- Improved soil management (cover crops, conservation tillage)
- Sustainable forest management (SFM)

**Land management changes that increase carbon sinks:**
- Agroforestry (trees integrated into agricultural systems)
- Restoration of degraded land
- Afforestation and reforestation
- Soil carbon sequestration

**Important distinction:** Land management changes that *reduce emissions* count toward FLAG SBTs. Carbon *removals* (sinks) do not count toward FLAG near-term SBTs but may count toward net-zero targets.

### 3.4 Demand-Side Approaches

The **FLAG Sector Pathway** is inherently a demand-side pathway — it reflects the global transformation needed in food systems, including:
- Dietary shifts toward lower-meat, plant-based diets
- Reduction of food loss and waste
- Improved food distribution and localization

**Companies can contribute to demand-side approaches through:**
- Product reformulation (lower-emission ingredients)
- Consumer engagement and labeling
- Food waste reduction programs
- Supply chain transparency

**Why demand-side?** The 12 GtCO₂e/yr global land sector mitigation potential by 2050 (Roe et al., 2019) includes approximately **0.9 GtCO₂e/yr from diet shifts** and **0.9 GtCO₂e/yr from food loss and waste reduction** — together nearly 15% of the total mitigation potential.

### 3.5 Carbon Removals

**What counts as FLAG carbon removals:**
- Forest carbon sequestration (regrowth, conservation)
- Soil organic carbon increases
- Agroforestry carbon sequestration
- Restored ecosystem carbon sinks

**Current treatment in FLAG SBTs:**
- Carbon removals **do not count toward near-term FLAG SBTs**.
- Carbon removals may be counted toward **net-zero long-term targets** (per the Corporate Net-Zero Standard).
- Carbon removals from land may be used for **neutralization of residual emissions** at the net-zero target year.
- Carbon credits from removals may be used for **BVCM** (Beyond Value Chain Mitigation) but NOT toward progress on science-based targets.

**This approach reflects:** The 38% of the 12 GtCO₂e/yr mitigation potential that comes from removals (forest restoration 14%, forestry/agroforestry 13%, soil carbon 11%) is acknowledged but not credited toward near-term corporate targets — instead, it is expected to be delivered through policy, landscape-level action, and market incentives.

### 3.6 Bioenergy

**Bioenergy and FLAG targets:**
- Bioenergy feedstock production (e.g., palm oil for biodiesel, sugarcane for ethanol) generates FLAG emissions (from land use, cultivation).
- These must be included in the FLAG target boundary.
- Both the biogenic CO₂ emissions from combustion AND the carbon sequestered during feedstock growth must be accounted for on a net basis.
- The net biogenic CO₂ from bioenergy feedstocks must be included in the FLAG SBT boundary.
- Required footnote: *"The target boundary includes land-related emissions and removals from biogenic feedstocks."*

### 3.7 Data Quality

**Data hierarchy (preferred to acceptable):**
1. Primary data (direct measurement, company-specific)
2. Tier 2 emission factors (country-specific, region-specific)
3. Tier 1 emission factors (global defaults, IPCC)
4. Proxy/spend-based data (last resort)

**Key data sources for FLAG emissions:**
- FAO GLEAM (livestock emissions)
- IPCC 2006 Guidelines for National GHG Inventories
- FAO FAOSTAT (land use, production data)
- GHG Protocol Agricultural Guidance
- Life cycle assessment databases (e.g., World Food LCA Database — ecoinvent, Agribalyse)

**Progressive improvement:**
Companies are expected to improve data quality over time. The FLAG Tool provides guidance on acceptable data sources and calculation methods. SBTi may require upgraded data quality at target revalidation.

### 3.8 Practical Steps for Setting FLAG Targets

**Step 1: Determine if your company must set a FLAG SBT**
- Calculate total scope 1 + 2 + 3 GHG emissions.
- Identify all FLAG-related emission sources.
- Calculate FLAG emissions as % of total.
- If ≥ 20%, FLAG SBT is mandatory (per FLAG-C1 V1.2).

**Step 2: Define the FLAG target boundary**
- Identify all FLAG emission sources within the organizational boundary.
- Determine scope coverage (scope 1+2 FLAG mandatory; scope 3 FLAG if ≥ 40% of total FLAG).
- Include LUC emissions.

**Step 3: Choose a FLAG pathway approach**
- FLAG Sector Pathway (3.03%/yr absolute)
- FLAG Commodity Pathways (per Table 8 / Section 4.1)
- Mixed approach

**Step 4: Gather data and use the FLAG Tool**
- Collect data as per Table 6.
- Download and use the SBTi FLAG Tool.
- Enter base year data and calculate required targets.

**Step 5: Formulate target language**
- Use Box 2 templates (per FLAG-C9).

**Step 6: Make no-deforestation commitment**
- Commit to no-deforestation by required date (≤ 2 years from submission, no later than Dec 31, 2030).
- Define cutoff date (best practice: ≤ 2020 or ≤ 3 years before first FLAG submission).

**Step 7: Submit to SBTi for validation**
- Through the SBTi Validation Portal.

### 4.2 Robust Science-Based 1.5°C Aligned Pathways

**Basis of the FLAG Sector Pathway:**

The FLAG sector pathway has been developed from:
> Roe, S., Streck, C., Obersteiner, M., Frank, S., Griscom, B., Drouet, L., Fricko, O., Gusti, M., Harris, N., Hasegawa, T., Hausfather, Z., Havlík, P., House, J., Nabuurs, G., Popp, A., Sanz Sánchez, M., Sanderman, J., Smith, P., & Stehfest, E., Lawrence, D. (2019). "Contribution of the land sector to a 1.5°C world." Nat Clim Change (9), 817-828. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41558-019-0591-9

Roe et al. (2019) compiled all available studies including:
- Shared Socioeconomic Pathway (SSP) Database (Rogelj et al., 2018)
- IAMC Database 1.5°C Scenario Explorer (Huppmann et al., 2018)
- Individual studies of 1.5°C carbon budgets (Rockström et al., 2017; Goodwin et al., 2018; Millar et al., 2017; Schurer et al., 2018; Tokarska & Gillett, 2018; Walsh et al., 2017)

The study was derived from four complementary analyses:
1. Review of 1.5°C scenarios across all sectors
2. Comparative analysis of top-down modeled pathways in the land sector
3. Bottom-up assessment and synthesis of land-sector mitigation potential
4. A geographically explicit road map of priority mitigation actions to fulfill the 1.5°C land-sector transformation pathway by 2050

**Basis of FLAG Commodity Pathways:**

The FLAG commodity pathways are described in:
> Smith, P., Nayak, D. R., Linthorst, G., Peters, D., Bucquet, C., Vuuren, D. P. V., Stehfest, E., Harmsen, M., & Brink, L. V. D. (2016). "Science-based GHG emissions targets for agriculture and forest commodities." University of Aberdeen, Ecofys, and PBL.

The commodity pathways are developed from the **IMAGE 3.0 Integrated Assessment Model**, which:
- Simulates global and regional environmental consequences of changes in human activities
- Considers 26 regions globally
- Covers FAO Statistical Databases (FAOSTAT) land use data (footnote 16: http://www.fao.org/faostat/en/#data)
- Oil crops (soybean and palm oil) were initially combined in IMAGE data then disaggregated based on FAO data

**Timber & wood fiber pathway:** Developed separately by **Quantis** using data from FAO Forest Resources Assessment (FRA) and other scientific literature for specific regional data. Full details available in the FLAG methods addendum.

### 4.3 How is Deforestation Addressed?

**Scale of the problem:**
- Deforestation-related emissions represent **12% of global emissions** and **50% of AFOLU emissions** (Roe et al., 2019).
- Both FLAG approaches (sector and commodity) consider the full mitigation potential of **3.6 GtCO₂e/yr** from reducing LUC emissions associated with deforestation.
- The FLAG sector pathway additionally includes LUC from conversion of natural ecosystems (an additional **1 GtCO₂e/yr** from reduction of LUC emissions).

**LUC emission allocation methodology:**
Deforestation causes GHG emissions (e.g., from biomass below- and above-ground, and soils) that can extend beyond the year when trees are cut. The SBTi FLAG methodology allocates deforestation emissions using **linear discounting over 20 years** following a deforestation event (see Figure 8 in document / Figure 13 below).

The 20-year LUC assessment period is commonly accepted based on:
- IPCC Good Practice Guidance for LULUCF (Penman et al., 2003)
- GHG Protocol (GHG Protocol, 2026)

Given these accounting principles and the science, the FLAG sector and FLAG commodity models **achieve zero deforestation by 2030**.

**Figure 13: Deforestation and Deforestation Emissions Pathways**
(Chart: "Allocated emissions follow tree cover loss by 20 years")
- X-axis: 2000 to 2055
- Tree cover loss line: 100% stable until ~2015, then rapid decline to 0% by 2030
- Allocated emissions line: 100% stable until ~2020, then gradual decline to 0% by 2050
- Adapted from Roe et al., 2019

Key interpretation: Even if tree cover loss stops by 2030, allocated emissions from that historical deforestation continue to be accounted for until 2050 (20-year linear discounting). This is why companies must act now on deforestation — the mitigation benefit accumulates slowly over the following 20 years.

> "Because deforestation must stop as soon as possible in alignment with the AFi guidance, the FLAG Guidance introduces additional criteria for companies to set no-deforestation goals (Section 3.2)."

## SECTION 5: GLOSSARY

A full list of SBTi terms, definitions, and acronyms is available in the **SBTi Glossary** (see sciencebasedtargets.org/resources).

**Key FLAG-specific terms:**

- **AFOLU:** Agriculture, Forestry and Other Land Use
- **dLUC:** Direct Land Use Change — emissions from land-use change directly linked to a specific company's activities
- **sLUC:** Statistical Land Use Change — LUC emissions allocated statistically based on commodity production
- **iLUC:** Indirect Land Use Change — displacement of emissions to other regions (not currently required in FLAG)
- **FLAG:** Forest, Land and Agriculture — the land-based sector encompassed by FLAG targets
- **FLAG SBT:** A science-based target specifically addressing FLAG-related emissions
- **FLAG Sector Pathway:** The 3.03%/yr absolute reduction pathway for overall FLAG emissions
- **FLAG Commodity Pathway:** Commodity-specific intensity reduction pathways
- **FPIC:** Free, Prior and Informed Consent — required from Indigenous Peoples and Local Communities for mitigation activities
- **GLEAM:** Global Livestock Environmental Assessment Model (FAO)
- **No-deforestation commitment:** Company commitment to eliminate deforestation from operations and supply chain by specified cutoff/target date
- **Cutoff date:** The date by which a company commits that deforestation in its supply chain has ceased; cannot be set later than best practice (≤ 2020)
- **SFM:** Sustainable Forest Management

## APPENDIX A: PRIMARY DEFORESTATION-LINKED COMMODITIES

### Table A.1: Primary Deforestation-Linked Commodities in the Context of the FLAG No-Deforestation Commitment

| Description | Inclusion in the No-Deforestation Commitment (FLAG-C4) |
|-------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| All commodities directly produced by the company. | They shall be included in the no-deforestation commitment regardless of their deforestation risk. |
| Commodities sourced by the company and contributing most significantly to deforestation globally, specifically cattle, cocoa, coffee, oil palm, rubber, soy, and timber.^16 This includes volumes of a commodity contained in transformed products where it makes up ≥ 1% of the finished sourced product and volumes of feed embedded in animal products. | These shall be included in a company's no-deforestation commitment if their sourced volumes, in tonnage, are ≥ 1% of the total sourced volume of FLAG commodities by the company.^17 |
| Commodities sourced by the company in significant volumes (≥ 5% of the total sourced volume, in tonnage, of FLAG commodities by the company). This includes volumes of a commodity contained in transformed products where it makes up ≥ 1% of the finished sourced product and volumes of feed embedded in animal products. | These shall be included in a company's no-deforestation commitment if they pose deforestation risks. |
| Commodities already assessed as deforestation-free by the company. | They shall be included in the no-deforestation commitment to ensure no-deforestation is maintained through time. |

^16 These are the commodities also included in the European Union Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) and that are amongst the most significant drivers of deforestation globally (Goldman et al., 2020).
^17 See CDP definition of sourced volumes.
